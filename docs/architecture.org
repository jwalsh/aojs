* Module Responsibilities

** =ao= (index.js) — User-Agent Classification

- Parse UA strings into device type, browser, OS
- Classify bots into 9 categories
- Lightweight, zero-dependency pattern matching
- No network calls, pure computation

** =ao/compliance= (compliance.js) — Bot Obligation Assessment

The core module. Answers: "what does this bot claim, and what does it owe?"

- Map UA → known bot profile (operator, policy URL, tier)
- Assess compliance against R1-R13 requirements
- Generate attestation statements (claims + obligations)
- Expose training vs. search token pairs for robots.txt decisions
- Document opt-out standards available to site operators

** =ao/log-analyzer= (log-analyzer.js) — Access Log Processing

- Parse Apache/Nginx combined log format
- Aggregate bot traffic by category
- Feed into compliance assessment for bulk analysis

* Compliance Verification Gaps

Current state: we classify bots and *state* their compliance level, but most
assessments are based on published claims, not verification. The gap:

| What we know | How we know it | Confidence |
|--------------|----------------|------------|
| UA identification (R1) | Observed in logs | High |
| robots.txt compliance (R2) | Operator claims | Medium |
| Rate limiting (R4) | Log analysis possible | Verifiable |
| Policy URL exists | HTTP fetch | Verifiable |
| Training vs search split | Published docs | Medium |
| Blocklist honoring (R3) | Need test infrastructure | Low |
| Backoff behavior (R5) | Need test infrastructure | Low |

* Social Platform Scrapers — Risk Assessment

These crawlers scrape content for preview/training and may store data indefinitely:

| Bot | Operator | Purpose | Data Retention | Concern |
|-----|----------|---------|---------------|---------|
| meta-externalagent | Meta | AI training | Indefinite | Training without consent |
| facebookexternalhit | Meta | Link previews | Cached | Low risk (preview only) |
| Twitterbot | X/Twitter | Card previews | Cached | Low risk (preview only) |
| LinkedInBot | LinkedIn | Link previews | Cached | Low risk |
| WhatsApp | Meta | Link previews | Cached | Low risk |
| PinterestBot | Pinterest | Image indexing | Indefinite | Image harvesting |
| Bytespider | ByteDance/TikTok | AI training | Indefinite | Aggressive, poor R1 |
| newsai | Unknown | Unknown | Unknown | No identification, no policy |

Key concern: **training crawlers** (meta-externalagent, Bytespider) differ from
**preview crawlers** (facebookexternalhit) — site operators should block the former
while allowing the latter for normal social sharing to work.

The TOKEN_PAIRS mapping makes this distinction actionable:
#+begin_src text
User-agent: meta-externalagent   # Block training
Disallow: /

User-agent: facebookexternalhit  # Allow previews
Allow: /
#+end_src
